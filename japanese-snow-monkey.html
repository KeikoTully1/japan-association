Discover Japanese snow monkeys

  • Date: Monday, March 6
  • Time: 6:30-7:30 pm ET
  • Where: Online
  • Cost: Free
RSVP

For this event, Zoologist professor Sakamoto will explain her research about Japanese snow monkeys in Japan.

Japanese snow monkeys, also known as Japanese macaques, are a type of monkey native to Japan. They are known for their thick fur coats and distinctive red faces.
They are called snow monkeys because they live in areas where snow covers the ground for several months each year.
One of the most famous behaviors of Japanese snow monkeys is their use of hot springs. They are the only non-human primates in the world known to regularly use hot springs for bathing and relaxation.

They are found in various habitats across Japan, including forests, mountains, and grasslands. Their range extends from the northern island of Hokkaido to the southern island of Kyushu.
